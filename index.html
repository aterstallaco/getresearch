Pioneering Biobehavioral Science in Cancer Survivorship

An interactive exploration of the research of Dr. Michael A. Hoyt, a leader in understanding the complex interplay between psychology, biology, and health in individuals facing chronic illness.

Core Focus

Biobehavioral processes of coping with chronic illness and cancer survivorship.

Key Innovation

Goal-focused Emotion-Regulation Therapy (GET) for young adult cancer survivors.

Primary Institution

Professor at University of California, Irvine (UCI).

From Theory to Application

Dr. Hoyt's research demonstrates a clear, programmatic progression from foundational coping theories to the development of evidence-based clinical interventions.

Emotional Approach Coping (EAC)

A foundational concept developed with Annette L. Stanton, EAC posits that the intentional use of emotional processing and expression is an adaptive strategy for managing adversity. This reframed emotion-focused coping, linking it to positive health behaviors and outcomes.

Biobehavioral Measurement

A hallmark of his work is the integration of psychological data with objective biological markers. By measuring salivary hormones (like cortisol) and inflammatory markers, his research provides physiological evidence for the impact of psychological states and interventions.

Translational Intervention

The culmination of his foundational work is the creation of clinical therapies like GET. This represents a translational leap, applying theoretical insights to solve real-world problems for cancer survivors and improve their quality of life.

Innovation in Action: Goal-focused Emotion-Regulation Therapy (GET)

GET is a novel, 6-session behavioral therapy designed to enhance self-regulation for young adult cancer survivors by targeting three key areas. It has demonstrated significant efficacy in improving mental health.
